Emil Martinec

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia


					Jump to:					navigation, 					search

Emil John Martinec (born October 4, 1958 in Downers Grove, Illinois) is an American string theorist born in 1958. He graduated from Northwestern University in 1979 and obtained his Ph.D. from Cornell University in 1984. He spent the last two years of his graduate education at SLAC after his dissertation advisor, Michael Peskin, left Cornell for SLAC. He is currently a professor at the Enrico Fermi Institute at the University of Chicago. He is a specialist in string theory.
References[edit]

The Heterotic String. David J. Gross, Jeffrey A. Harvey, Emil J. Martinec, Ryan Rohm, Published in Phys. Rev. Lett. 54:502–505, 1985.
